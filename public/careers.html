Bookkeeper and Payroll Manager

Job Overview: As a Bookkeeper and Payroll Manager, you will be responsible for managing day-to-day financial transactions, maintaining accurate records, and overseeing payroll processes. Your role will ensure that our clients’ financials and employee compensations are handled efficiently and in compliance with tax regulations and best practices.

Key Responsibilities:
Bookkeeping:
Record day-to-day financial transactions and complete the posting process.
Reconcile accounts, bank statements, and maintain general ledgers.
Manage accounts payable and receivable.
Generate financial reports, including profit and loss statements, and balance sheets.
Prepare and process month-end and year-end closings.
Payroll Management:
Process payroll for multiple clients, ensuring accuracy and timeliness.
Maintain payroll records and prepare payroll reports.
Handle payroll tax filings and ensure compliance with local, state, and federal tax regulations.
Manage employee benefits and deductions where applicable.
Address payroll-related inquiries from clients or employees.
General Duties:
Collaborate with team members to support client financial operations.
Ensure compliance with company policies and relevant accounting standards.
Stay updated on changes in tax laws and payroll regulations.

Qualifications and Skills:
Bachelor’s degree in Accounting, Finance, or a related field (preferred but not mandatory).
Proven experience in bookkeeping and payroll processing (minimum 2–3 years).
Proficiency in accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks, Xero) and payroll systems.
Strong knowledge of tax regulations and accounting principles.
Exceptional attention to detail and organizational skills.
Excellent communication and interpersonal skills.
Ability to multitask and meet deadlines in a fast-paced environment.
Certification in bookkeeping or payroll management (preferred).

What We Offer:

  • Competitive salary.
  • Opportunities for professional growth and development.
  • A supportive and collaborative work environment.

Account Administrator

Job Overview: As an Account Administrator, you will play a key role in handling client accounts, managing administrative tasks, and supporting the bookkeeping and payroll teams. Your organizational skills and attention to detail will help maintain the accuracy and efficiency of financial operations while ensuring client satisfaction.

Key Responsibilities:
Act as the main point of contact for client inquiries and provide timely support.
Maintain and update client records in the accounting database.
Assist with accounts receivable and payable, including issuing invoices and tracking payments.
Prepare and send financial reports, statements, and correspondence to clients.
Collect and organize financial documentation from clients for processing.
Support the bookkeeping team with data entry, reconciliations, and other administrative tasks.
Coordinate client onboarding by gathering necessary documents and setting up systems.
Monitor and track important deadlines for compliance, filings, and payments.
Schedule meetings, maintain calendars, and support internal team communication.

Qualifications and Skills:
Proven experience in an administrative or accounts-related role (minimum 1–2 years).
Familiarity with bookkeeping and basic accounting principles (preferred).
Proficiency in accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks, Xero) and Microsoft Office Suite.
Strong organizational and multitasking skills with excellent attention to detail.
Effective verbal and written communication skills.
Ability to manage confidential information with professionalism.
A proactive attitude and problem-solving mindset.
Bachelor’s degree in Business Administration, Accounting, or a related field (preferred but not required).

    What We Offer:
  • Competitive salary.
  • Opportunities for professional growth and development.
  • A supportive and collaborative work environment.
